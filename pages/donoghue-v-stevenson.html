Donoghue v Stevenson (1932)

Home

Case Summary

Mrs Donoghue found a decomposed snail in a bottle of ginger beer purchased by a friend. She sued the manufacturer, Mr Stevenson, for negligence after suffering illness.

Legal Principle

The case established the neighbour principle: one must take reasonable care to avoid acts or omissions that could foreseeably harm others. This became a cornerstone of modern negligence law.

Key Takeaways

  • Introduced modern law of negligence.
  • Defined duty of care between manufacturers and consumers.
  • Precedent is binding in similar cases.
Q: How does the neighbour principle affect modern tort law?
A: It requires individuals and organizations to take reasonable care to avoid harming others, forming the basis for negligence claims.
