Meaning of Law

Law refers to the system of rules that governs society, designed to maintain order, resolve disputes, and protect rights. It provides a framework for acceptable behavior and consequences for violations.

Q: Why is law necessary in society?
A: Law is necessary to prevent chaos, protect individual rights, and maintain social order.

Customs, Rules, and Law

Customs are long-standing social practices; rules are more formal expectations in communities or organizations, while laws are enforced by legal institutions with formal penalties.

Characteristics of Just Laws

Just laws are fair, uphold equality, allow access to justice, and reflect society’s values. They ensure fairness in enforcement and decision-making.

Nature of Justice

Justice involves equality (treating everyone fairly), fairness (impartiality in decisions), and access (everyone can reach legal remedies).

Rule of Law

The rule of law means everyone, including the government, is subject to the law. It prevents arbitrary power and ensures accountability.
