Division of Powers

The Constitution divides powers between federal and state governments. This ensures clear responsibilities, preventing one level from overstepping authority.

Separation of Powers

Power is separated between legislative, executive, and judicial branches to prevent abuse of power. Each branch has independent functions but must cooperate for effective governance.

Role of the High Court

The High Court interprets the Constitution and resolves disputes between governments. Its decisions guide legal understanding across Australia and establish binding precedents.
