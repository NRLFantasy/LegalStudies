Domestic vs International Law

Domestic law governs within a country; international law governs relations between nations. State sovereignty ensures each state can enforce its laws independently while adhering to treaties.

Sources of International Law

International law comes from customary practices, treaties, declarations, and judicial decisions. These sources guide state behaviour and ensure accountability globally.

Organisations

The United Nations, courts, tribunals, IGOs, and NGOs play a crucial role in implementing and enforcing international law. They monitor compliance and facilitate global cooperation.

Relevance to Australia

International law influences Australian law through ratified treaties and customs, impacting trade, human rights, and environmental policies. Courts sometimes reference international law to interpret domestic statutes.
