Common Law

Common law originates from British law and is developed through judicial decisions. It includes the principle of precedent, where past decisions guide future ones, and the adversarial system in trials ensures fairness in presenting evidence.

Q: What is the role of precedent in common law?
A: Precedent ensures consistency, where courts follow previous decisions to create predictable outcomes.

Court Hierarchy

The court hierarchy determines the authority and jurisdiction of each court. State and federal courts handle cases differently, with higher courts able to overrule lower courts.

Statute Law

Parliament creates statutes, which are written laws that govern society. The legislative process ensures laws are carefully considered, and delegated legislation allows bodies to make rules under parliamentary authority.
